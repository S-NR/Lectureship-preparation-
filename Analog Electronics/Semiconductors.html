Semiconductor Basics

1. Energy Band Theory

In solids, electrons occupy specific energy levels forming bands. The two important bands are: Valence Band and Conduction Band. The energy difference between them is called Band Gap (Eg).

Material Band Gap Example
Conductor 0 eV Copper
Semiconductor ~1 eV Silicon
Insulator Large Glass

2. Intrinsic Semiconductor

Pure semiconductor material without any impurity is called intrinsic semiconductor. Number of electrons = Number of holes.

3. Extrinsic Semiconductor (Doping)

When impurities are added to improve conductivity, it is called doping.

N-Type Semiconductor

Formed by adding pentavalent impurity (e.g., Phosphorus). Majority carriers are electrons.

P-Type Semiconductor

Formed by adding trivalent impurity (e.g., Boron). Majority carriers are holes.

4. Important Formulas

  • Current Density: J = nqμE
  • Drift Current: I = nqμAE
  • Conductivity: σ = nqμ
  • Mass Action Law: np = ni²

5. Exam Important Points

  • Band gap of Silicon ≈ 1.1 eV at room temperature
  • Intrinsic Fermi level lies near mid-gap
  • In N-type, Fermi level shifts towards conduction band
  • In P-type, Fermi level shifts towards valence band
